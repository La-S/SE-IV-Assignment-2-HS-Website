Soft Skills

Soft Skills are often related to the work you'll do as a computer scientist, but aren't directly related to technology usage. Employers still value these!
Just like any other skills, the best way to improve is to practice!

Some example soft skills (there are more) are:

An incredibly important skill for a computer scientist! For many careers in the industry, problem solving is essential. Many developers think of their work as continually solving small problems, until eventually the app is made.

Working with software often requires attention to detail. Computers are very exact, unlike humans, and will not correct for slight errors. Computer Scientists need to be able to accurately identify and fix problems with obscure edge-cases.

Many jobs will have you work with new technologies, languages, frameworks, or entirely new responsibilities. Technologies evolve over time, and you may entirely change job fields. Being able to identify similar patterns and learning new technologies quickly will be a great help! Practicing this skill mostly comes down to learning more and more technologies, and trying to draw connections between them.

Most computer scientists work in teams, so being able to work with others quickly and effectively will be a great help to you and any future supervisor.

Like other job fields, you'll often have to present your work and findings to another colleague, your team, a client, or another third party. Knowing how to create a presentation and deliver it effectively can be great for displaying your skills to others.

Computer Scientists often have changing requirements, inflexible clients, and a whole host of other problems. Being able to be adaptable to changes will greatly improve your happiness and productivity.
